The Biodiversity of Deep-Water Coral Environments

There are many species of deep-water coral and species which use deep-water coral environments as their habitat. Lophelia communities support diverse marine life, such as sponges, polychaete worms, mollusks, crustaceans, brittle stars, starfish, sea urchins, bryozoans, sea spiders, fish, and many other vertebrate and invertebrate species.

Lophelia Pertusa

One of the most common of deep-water coral, Lophelia pertusa, lives in the Northeast and Northwest Atlantic Ocean, Brazil and off Africa’s west coast.
